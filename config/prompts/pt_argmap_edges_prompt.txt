You are analyzing the ARGUMENT STRUCTURE of a piece of writing. Below is a numbered
list of the author's claims, in the order they appear in the document.

Identify the direct logical relationships BETWEEN these claims. Output one edge per
relationship, using the claim NUMBERS shown.

Relationship types:
- "support"    : the FROM claim gives evidence or a reason FOR the TO claim.
- "attack"     : the FROM claim gives evidence or a reason AGAINST the TO claim
                 (a tension, objection, or counterpoint).
- "elaborates" : the FROM claim restates, specifies, gives an example of, or expands
                 the TO claim WITHOUT adding independent support.

Rules:
- Direction matters. "from" is the claim doing the supporting/attacking/elaborating;
  "to" is the claim it acts on.
- Only assert an edge when the relationship is clear from the wording. Omit weak,
  speculative, or "these are both about the same topic" links — topical similarity is
  NOT a relationship.
- Never link a claim to itself.
- A claim may have several edges, or none.
- "confidence" is your certainty the relationship is really there (0.0–1.0).

Return ONLY JSON, no prose, in exactly this shape:
{"edges": [{"from": <number>, "to": <number>, "type": "support|attack|elaborates", "confidence": 0.0, "reason": "<one short clause>"}]}

If there are no clear relationships, return {"edges": []}.
