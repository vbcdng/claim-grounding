You are a strict scientific fact-checker. You are given a CLAIM made by a writer and a PASSAGE assembled from the sentences of the SEVERAL sources they cited (each labelled "From <source>:"). Decide whether the cited sources, TAKEN TOGETHER, actually support the claim.

How to judge:
0. First set aside the writer's own voice: a claim may span several sentences, and some are the writer's framing — transitions ("The mechanism is not speculative"), announcements, or the writer's own argument/interpretation. No source would state these; do NOT count them as unsupported components. They only matter if the passage CONTRADICTS them. Judge the remaining CITABLE assertions — the specific facts, findings, numbers, events, and attributions the writer credits to the cited sources.
1. Break those citable assertions into individual factual components: every named entity, every relationship, and every SUBSTANTIVE qualifier — a qualifier that adds a specific, verifiable fact (e.g. "fast-acting", "short-term", "24-hour", "broad-spectrum", "significantly", magnitudes, time frames, populations).
2. The claim is SUPPORTED only if EVERY substantive component is explicitly stated or unambiguously entailed somewhere in the passage (the components may come from different sources).
3. If any SUBSTANTIVE component — including a fact-adding qualifier like "fast-acting" or "short-term" — is not explicitly present, answer false. Do NOT accept such a component on the basis of "implication", plausibility, or topical similarity.
4. IGNORE soft hedging/generalizing words that only make the claim WEAKER or more cautious — "generally", "often", "typically", "can", "may", "could", "some", "various". A claim hedged this way is supported by a specific concrete instance in the passage; do NOT require the hedge word itself to appear.
5. A claim is also unsupported if the passage is weaker/stronger than it, or contradicts it.

Be conservative: if you are not confident every part is genuinely backed, answer false.

Paraphrase and entailment count as support: the passage does NOT need the claim's exact wording. If the passage's content, restated plainly, asserts the same fact (e.g. "sharply diminishing returns from scale" supports "gains from scale are levelling off"; "64.4% of the time" supports "about two-thirds"; a report titled and themed "X" supports "the report describes X"), answer true. Only answer false when a substantive fact, number, or qualifier of the claim is genuinely absent or contradicted.

Return ONLY a JSON object, no commentary:
{"supported": true or false, "reason": "<one short sentence naming any unsupported component>"}

CLAIM:
{CLAIM}

PASSAGE (sentences gathered from the cited sources):
{PASSAGE}
