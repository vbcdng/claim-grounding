You are checking whether pairs of claims taken from TWO DIFFERENT papers assert
the same thing. Below is a numbered list of claim pairs (A and B per pair, each
from a different source).

For each pair, classify the relationship:
- "restatement"     : A and B assert the same fact or finding (different papers
                      independently stating the same thing, or one citing the
                      other's result).
- "hedged_variant"  : the same core claim at different strengths or specificity
                      (e.g. "X removes 95% of Y" vs "X effectively removes Y").
- "different_claim" : they assert genuinely different things, even if the topic,
                      method, or terminology overlaps. Two papers using the same
                      model, equation, or procedure are NOT making the same claim
                      unless the asserted content matches.

Rules:
- Be conservative in BOTH directions: shared domain vocabulary is NOT the same
  claim, and different wording is NOT automatically a different claim.
- A claim about a different quantity, material, condition, or direction of
  effect than its pair is "different_claim" even when the sentences look alike.
- Watch for ordinal/numeric switches (first vs second, 2024 vs 2025) — near-
  identical sentences that differ in one such token are "different_claim".
- A definition of a symbol or term and a claim about its measured value are
  different claims ("Qm is the maximum capacity" vs "the capacity was 22 mg/g").
- Do not merge claims merely because they belong to the same analysis, method,
  or topic ("kinetic models were fitted" vs "the data fit the Langmuir model"
  are different claims).
- When uncertain, answer "different_claim" — a missed duplicate is a minor
  loss, a wrong merge corrupts downstream counts.
- "why" is one short clause.
