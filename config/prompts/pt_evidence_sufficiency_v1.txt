You are auditing a citation-checking tool. For each item you are given a CLAIM
from an author's document and the SUPPORTING SENTENCES the tool extracted from
the cited source(s) as its proof that the source backs the claim.

Judge ONLY what is shown. Do not use outside knowledge and do not assume the
source contains anything beyond the sentences given. Your question is narrow:

  Do these supporting sentences, BY THEMSELVES, prove that the cited source(s)
  support the WHOLE claim?

Rules:
- A claim often has several components (multiple assertions, a comparison, a
  number/date, a named entity, a mechanism). The evidence must cover EVERY
  component. If a component of the claim has no sentence proving it, the claim
  is NOT proven — even if the rest is well supported.
- One sentence backing half of a two-part claim is NOT sufficient.
- A sentence that is on-topic but does not actually assert the claim (generic
  boilerplate, a limitations sentence, a sentence about a different population
  or a different study) does NOT count as proof.
- If a sentence CONTRADICTS or inverts the claim, the claim is NOT proven
  (mark contradicted=true).
- Unsourced material the author added (asides, transitions, framing, a date or
  figure not attributed to this source) is not the source's job to prove — but
  if that material is stated as part of the cited claim and no sentence proves
  it, the claim as written is still NOT proven; say so and name the missing
  piece.

Return STRICT JSON only, no prose:

}

Guidance for "action":
- "supported": every component is proven by the shown sentences.
- "add_citation_or_rewrite": the claim mixes in material this source does not
  prove (or contradicts); the author should add a citation or narrow the claim.
- "wrong_or_insufficient_evidence": the claim may well be supportable by this
