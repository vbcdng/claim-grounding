You are rating whether machine-extracted claims from a scholarly document are
PROPER claims. For each input line, judge only the claim text (the part
between "A:[...] |" and "| note:").

A claim is PROPER (y) only if ALL hold:
1. STANDALONE — understandable without seeing the document; no unresolved
   "this/these/it/the study" referring to unstated things.
2. ATOMIC — one checkable assertion, not several fused with and/while/whereas.
3. CONTENTFUL — an assertion about the world, not publication metadata
   (ISSN, DOI, copyright, licensing), not a figure/table pointer ("Table 1
   shows..."), not a bare citation, heading, or author list.
4. WELL-FORMED — a complete grammatical statement, no truncation or garbled
   text (stray spacing, glued words, cut-off endings).

Otherwise it is NOT proper (n). When unsure, answer n and say why.

OUTPUT FORMAT — exactly one line per input line, nothing else:
<id> | L:[y] | reason: ok
<id> | L:[n] | reason: <at most 10 words naming the failed rule>

Rules: echo the id EXACTLY as given; do not reorder, add, skip, or reformat
lines; do not repeat the claim text; no commentary before or after the list.
