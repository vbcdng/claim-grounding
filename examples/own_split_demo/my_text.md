This essay argues that protecting sleep is the most underrated lever for knowledge work.

Consider the underlying mechanism.

During deep sleep the brain clears metabolic waste through the glymphatic system, a process that is far less active during waking hours. [[sleep]]

Adults who sleep fewer than six hours a night score roughly 30% worse on sustained-attention tasks the following day.

In my experience, the mid-afternoon slump is the clearest signal that the previous night's sleep was cut too short.

Therefore, guarding a full night's sleep is not an indulgence but the single highest-leverage productivity habit available to most people.
